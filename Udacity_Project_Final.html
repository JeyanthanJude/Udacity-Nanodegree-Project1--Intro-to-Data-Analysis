Sean Lahman's Baseball data¶

I have analyzed the baseball data in the http://www.seanlahman.com website. The site contains the complete baseball statistics from 1871 to 2016 in various datasets. I have analyzed the 'Salaries', 'Teams','AllstarFull','Batting;,'Pitching','HallofFame' csv files in specific.

Questions?¶

  1. How has the attendance varied from year to year?
  2. What is the trend of the average salary for a baseball player over the years?
  3. How does the average salary of all star players compare to non allstar players?
  4. Which league, the National league or the American league has won the most number of world series title?
  5. What percent of people nominated for the Hall of Fame finally get voted into it?
  6. How are the performance stats for a player in the Hall of Fame? etc.

1.How has the attendance varied from year to year?¶

In the Teams.csv file, among many stats is the home attendance total for a particular team. How has the attendance varied from year to year? To answer this question, the mean home attendance per year is analyzed.

Limitations:¶

  1. The salaries of players in the Salaries.csv file were recorded only from the year 1985. The salaries from the previous years were missing. So the mean salary curve could look different from the one in the report had those values been included.
  2. Batting and Pitching stats of certain players were missing. Especially those from leagues like the Negro league. Stats of Hall of Famers from these leagues couldn't be analyzed. Hence the Batting Average and the ERA could have differed from the once mentioned in the report.
  3. Removing the names of Hall of Famers with no batting or pitching records caused additional time and also needed additional code.

Conclusion:¶

  1. The average league attendance has increased over the years but has also witnessed sudden drop offs in certain years.
  2. The avearge salary of basball ball players has increased almost exponentially over the years.
  3. The average salary of a player who has been in an all star game atleast once is 5.2 times higher than the one who hasn;t been to the game.
  4. The American league has won the most number of world series title.
  5. Only 7.63% of the total number of people nominated to the Hall of Fame are finally nominated into it.
  6. Players aren't the only once inducted into the Hall of Fame. Pioneers/Executives, Umpires, managers are also inducted.
  7. Players in the Hall of Fame tend to have a higher Batting Average or a lower ERA.
